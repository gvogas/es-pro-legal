1. Who this policy covers

The App is used by two kinds of people:

  • Account holders — the business owner/administrator and the employees they invite, who sign in to use the App.
  • Clients — the customers of a business that uses the App, whose contact and appointment details are entered into the App by the account holders. Clients do not sign in to the App themselves.

A business that uses the App to manage its own customers is responsible for the client information it enters and decides why and how that information is collected and used. In that context, we act as a service provider (processor) that stores and processes the information on the business’s behalf and under this policy. If you are a client of such a business and have questions about your information, please contact that business directly; you may also contact us using the details below.

2. Information we collect

We collect only the information needed to operate the App. We do not require you to provide information that is not necessary for the features you use.

2.1 Information you provide

CategoryExamplesPurpose
Account information Email address, password, display name, phone number, and role (administrator or employee). To create and secure your account and control access.
Client records (entered by account holders) Client name, phone number, email address, and postal/street address. To schedule and manage appointments for the business’s customers.
Appointment content Date and time, assigned employees, status, notes, and location. To provide the core scheduling features.
Photos Images you take or select to attach to appointments or profiles. To store and display images you choose to add.

Passwords are managed by Firebase Authentication (see below) using industry standard hashing. We never receive or store your password in plain text.

2.2 Device features and permissions

These are used only when you choose to use the related feature:

  • Camera — to take appointment or profile photos.
  • Photo library — to attach existing images.
  • Contacts — to open a pre-filled contact card so you can save a client to your phone’s contacts. The App does not read, collect, or upload your device’s contact list.
  • Face ID / biometrics — to unlock the optional in-app lock you can enable in Settings. Biometric data is processed entirely by iOS on your device and never leaves it; we do not receive or store it.

2.3 Information collected automatically

  • Diagnostics and crash data. We use Firebase Crashlytics to collect crash reports and limited technical diagnostics (for example, device model, operating system version, and the technical state of the App at the time of a crash) so that we can identify and fix problems.
  • App integrity signals. To protect the service, the App uses Apple App Attest and Firebase App Check to verify that requests come from a genuine, untampered copy of the App. This process uses a device-generated attestation and does not identify you personally.
  • Address lookups. When you type an address, the partial text is sent, through our secure backend, to Google Maps Platform (Places API) to return address suggestions.

The App does not use third-party advertising SDKs, does not collect precise background location, and does not track you across other companies’ apps or websites.

3. How and why we use information

We use personal information to:

  • create, secure, and operate accounts, and authenticate users;
  • provide the core scheduling features — appointments, clients, and employees — and control which appointments each user can see;
  • store and display the records and photos you create;
  • provide address autocomplete when you enter an address;
  • diagnose crashes, maintain security, and improve reliability; and
  • comply with legal obligations and respond to lawful requests.

Legal basis. We process personal information on the basis of your consent, to perform the service you request, for our legitimate business interest in operating and securing the App, and to meet legal obligations. For client information entered by a business, the business is responsible for having the appropriate basis to collect and use that information. You may withdraw consent as described in Section 8; withdrawing consent may limit features that depend on the information.

5. Where information is stored (international transfers)

Personal information is stored and processed on cloud infrastructure operated by our service providers, which may be located outside your province or country, including in the United States. As a result, your information may be subject to the laws of those jurisdictions, including lawful access by government authorities. We rely on our providers’ contractual and technical safeguards to protect information that is transferred or stored outside Quebec and Canada. By using the App, you acknowledge that your information may be processed in these locations.

6. How long we keep information

  • Account information is retained while your account is active. When you delete your account, your account record and profile are removed (see Section 8).
  • Client, appointment, and photo records created within a business remain part of that business’s records for as long as the business keeps them, and are deleted when the business deletes them.
  • Crash diagnostics are retained by Firebase Crashlytics for a limited period in accordance with Google’s policies.

We keep personal information only as long as necessary for the purposes described in this policy or as required by law, after which it is deleted or anonymized.

7. How we protect information

  • Information is encrypted in transit (HTTPS/TLS) and encrypted at rest by our cloud providers.
  • Access is restricted by security rules enforced on the backend: an employee can see only the appointments assigned to them, and administrative functions require an administrator account.
  • Backend requests require Apple App Attest / Firebase App Check verification, which blocks requests from unverified or tampered clients.
  • Sensitive server-side keys are stored in a managed secrets service and are never included in the App.

No method of transmission or storage is completely secure, but we take reasonable measures to protect personal information against loss, theft, and unauthorized access, use, or disclosure.

8. Your rights and choices

Subject to applicable law, you have the right to access, correct, and delete your personal information, to withdraw consent, and to obtain information about how your information is handled. Residents of Quebec and elsewhere in Canada may have additional rights under the Private Sector Act (Law 25) and PIPEDA.

  • Delete your account. You can delete your account and associated profile at any time from within the App (Settings → Account → Delete account). This is available in the App and does not require contacting us.
  • Access and correction. You can view and edit much of your information directly in the App. To request access to, or correction of, other personal information we hold about you, contact us using the details below.
  • Deletion and withdrawal of consent. You may ask us to delete your personal information or withdraw your consent to certain processing. Some information may be retained where required by law.
  • Device permissions. You can grant or revoke camera, photo, contacts, and Face ID permissions at any time in your device’s Settings; features that rely on a permission will be limited if it is revoked.
  • If you are a client of a business that uses the App, please direct access, correction, and deletion requests to that business, which controls its records; you may also contact us and we will assist or refer your request as appropriate.

We will respond to verified requests within the time required by applicable law. We may need to verify your identity before acting on a request.

9. Tracking, advertising, and automated decisions

The App does not track you across other companies’ apps or websites, does not use third-party advertising, and does not sell personal information. The App does not use your personal information to make decisions about you based solely on automated processing. This policy page itself uses no cookies or analytics trackers.

10. Children’s privacy

The App is a business tool intended for use by adults. It is not directed to children, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information, please contact us so we can delete it.

11. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we will update the “Last updated” date above and, where appropriate, provide notice through the App or by other means. Your continued use of the App after an update takes effect constitutes acceptance of the revised policy.
